# Teaching notes — Alfred email triage

## Learner profile
- Expert engineer; author of most of this codebase. Teach dense, precise, terminology-forward. No hand-holding.
- Wants vocabulary + as-is flow as scaffolding for an architecture decision (the "user standing" gap).
- Mode is design thinking, not bug-fixing line by line.

## How they like it
- Code-grounded: every claim cites file:line in this repo. No parametric/generic theory.
- Tufte-style reference docs they'll revisit. Lessons are disposable; the glossary is durable.
- Lead with the rubric/principle, exemplars only at boundaries (matches their own stated triage-design philosophy: "principles over exemplars").

## Recurring theme to exploit in lessons
- The unifying lens: **what user-context does each consumer read?** Triage reads sender-side relationship signal (graph-derived) but NOT the cold-start brief / bio / facts. That asymmetry IS the mission.
