Why a post-model net, not a prompt edit

You could tell the model to weigh standing. But the override floor exists precisely because some judgments are too important to leave to a cheap model's discretion — you encode them deterministically. Demote-never-bury is exactly that kind of invariant: it must never bury, and a prompt can't guarantee "never." So B belongs in the same layer as the floor — a hand-written net with a hard floor, not a rubric line the model may ignore. (This also matches your own principle: rubric for judgment, code for invariants.)
