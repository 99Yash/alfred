The triage flow, and where your standing should enter

One email becomes one thread tag. Trace it once, and the gap you're chasing locates itself.

Triage is an LLM call wrapped in deterministic nets. The model is cheap and replaceable; the intelligence lives in what context you assemble before it runs and what guards you wrap around it after. Your mission lives in the first half — the context.

The flow in seven beats

  1. Ingest → event. Gmail messages land in documents; emitGmailMessageEvents() fires a gmail.message_received event. packages/api/src/modules/integrations/queue.ts:367
  2. Workflow wakes. The email-triage workflow is event-sourced and starts at the classify step. apps/server/src/builtins/workflows/email-triage.ts:119
  3. Sender context (free). extractSenderContext() parses the header/body — person vs service, bot slug. No model, no cost. sender-context.ts:47 · ADR-0042
  4. Observations (deterministic reads). gatherObservations() fans out: sender prior, thread state, known-contact, and the senderRelationship descriptor. email-triage.ts:258 · observations.ts:137
  5. Classify (the cheap model). classifyEmail() sends SYSTEM_PROMPT + observations to Flash Lite → {category, confidence, rationale, todoSuggestion}. classify.ts:737
  6. Guards. detectConflict() may trigger a second cheap pass; applyOverrideFloor() can force urgent on an exposed secret. classify.ts:463, 505
  7. Persist & reconcile. upsertTriage() writes the thread-keyed row; reconcileThreadLabel() collapses the thread to one Gmail label; a todo may be proposed. store.ts:127 · tags.ts:52

Every noun above is defined in the glossary. Beat 4 is the one that matters for you.

Your example, traced

LinkedIn message from a junior job-seeker → awaiting_reply

Beat 3: fromKind = person, no bot slug. A human.
Beat 4: senderRelationship resolves to roughly "no prior contact" — graph says nothing, because there is no history.
Beat 4: thread state → newest message is inbound, you haven't replied → looks like they're waiting on you.
Beat 5: Model sees: a human, inbound, unanswered, no contradicting prior. Reasonable call → awaiting_reply.
Result: surfaced as if it deserves your attention. The model was not wrong given its inputs — the inputs were incomplete.

The seam. Notice what the classifier never saw: who you are. Every input is sender-side (who they are) or thread-side (the conversation's shape). Nothing encodes your absolute standing — that a founder/senior IC does not owe a reply to a cold junior ask. That signal is supposed to come from cold-start research, and today it is not plumbed into observations at all.

So the fix is not "make the model smarter." It is: add a user-standing field to the Observations bundle (beat 4) and teach the rubric to discount inbound asks that fall below your threshold. The model is already the right size; it's starving on context.
