--- notes ---
- While the iframes are streaming HTML in, their RootWebArea is marked `busy`. Once the page finishes loading, the `busy` flag clears. So a page row whose iframe RootWebArea is `busy` corresponds to a chat-side status of "Creating <title> page...".
- Page rows in the panel use the same titles the LLM emits in the chat status stream — they are stamped twice (panel strip + in-document footer "0N / 06"), so the title and the page itself are decoupled and named before the body resolves.
- The right panel's empty state ("No Pages Yet" / "Pages appear here as they're generated") is replaced in-place by the first resolved page; pages append below.
- Streaming status `StaticText`s sit inline among the tool buttons / regions in the assistant message — they are not their own region. So when a status completes, the same text node is replaced (e.g. "Creating <title> page..." → "Created <title> page.").
- The top-level "Working on it..." heading stays until the run finishes (after the final tool — email — completes). It's the only run-level status indicator; per-step progress is the inline stream described above.
